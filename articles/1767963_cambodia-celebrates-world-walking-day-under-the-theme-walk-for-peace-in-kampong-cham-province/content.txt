Cambodia celebrated World Walking Day with the theme “Walk for Peace,” emphasising the vital importance of peace yesterday.

The event, led by Tourism Minister Huot Hak and Kampong Cham Governor UN Chanda, aimed to promote tourism during the Green Season and showcase the province’s potential. Around 6,000 people took part, including 250 monks, local leaders, students, and citizens.

In his speech, Minister Hak emphasised the importance of peace, national unity, and solidarity achieved under the leadership of Former Prime Minister Hun Sen.

He said that this legacy is being maintained by Prime Minister Hun Manet.

The Minister also highlighted the role of sports tourism in enhancing Cambodia’s offerings, stating that events like World Walking Day help diversify the tourism sector and attract both local and international visitors.
